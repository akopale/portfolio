Maximum Temperature in Iowa 2013-2016

The four maps on this page show the maximum temperature across Iowa from 2013-2016. These maps rely on data from the National Weather Service Cooperative Observer (COOP) network in Iowa. This is a network of volunteers who make and report daily weather observations. Many important pieces of weather and climatological data would be unknown if this network of people did not provide this invaluable information. You can see that, generally, the maximum temperature in Iowa has increased significantly over the past four years. In both 2013 and 2014, the red color representing the highest class of temperatures, 61.798 - 65.420, does not even appear on the maps. In 2015 and 2016, the same is true for the pale yellow color, the class representing the lowest maximum temperatures (50.93 - 54.553) is absent. This shows that the maximum temperature in Iowa has increased over four years.
